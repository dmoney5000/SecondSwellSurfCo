Lessons FAQ

Before you paddle out

How far is Cocoa Beach from Orlando?
About 50–60 minutes from downtown Orlando or the theme parks via the 528 Beachline — Cocoa Beach is the closest beach to Orlando. Morning lesson, back by early afternoon.
Will I actually stand up on my first lesson?
Most students do. Cocoa Beach waves are small, slow, and break over sand — the most forgiving learning conditions on the East Coast. With an instructor pushing you into waves on a high-volume soft top, most first-timers ride to the beach within the hour.
Do I need to bring anything?
Just swimwear, sunscreen, and a towel. Boards, leashes, and rash guards are included, and wetsuits are provided free roughly December through March.
What's the minimum age, and do parents need to book a spot?
Ages 6 and up, swimming ability required. Kids 12 and under get one-on-one water supervision. Parents watch free from the sand — bring a chair and a camera.
I can already surf. Do you coach intermediates?
Yes — our intermediate sessions cover catching unbroken green waves, trimming and turning, lineup positioning, and honest equipment advice (often the fix is the board, and we have a rack full of options to try).
