Why it matters

A ding is a leak with a deadline

The problem

Water kills foam

Any crack through the glass wicks saltwater into the core. A waterlogged board gets heavier, browner, and weaker every session — and a $45 fix becomes a $200 one.

The fix

Matched materials

PU boards get polyester resin; EPS boards get epoxy — never crossed, because polyester resin melts EPS foam. Cloth weight is matched to your glassing schedule so flex stays true.

The finish

Sanded fair, not just sealed

A repair you can feel under your hand will track water and slow you down. Ours are faired into the rocker line and finished gloss or matte to match the rest of the board.

Repair FAQ

Common questions

How long does surfboard repair take?
Most dings, cracks, and fin boxes: 3–5 days. Buckles, snaps, and restorations: 1–2 weeks. Rush turnaround in 24–48 hours is available for most small repairs.
Can a snapped surfboard be repaired?
Usually, yes. A clean snap or buckle can be structurally rebuilt with rod reinforcement and new glass, restoring most of the board's strength. If the math doesn't work, we'll say so — and offer trade credit toward a board on our used rack instead.
Should I keep surfing with a small ding?
Tape it watertight for a session if you have to, but get it fixed soon. Open cracks waterlog the foam, which adds weight and turns small repairs into structural ones.
Do you repair epoxy boards, soft tops, SUPs, and foil boards?
Epoxy and soft tops, yes. SUPs and foil boards case-by-case depending on construction — send photos and we'll tell you straight.
