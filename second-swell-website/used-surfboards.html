How we grade every used board

Grade A

Barely surfed

Light pressure dings at most, zero repairs or one professional repair, no discoloration, full strength. The closest thing to new without the new price.

Grade B

Surfed and solid

Normal wear, professionally repaired dings (all disclosed), watertight and structurally sound. The sweet spot of price and performance — most of our rack lives here.

Grade C

Honest beaters

Cosmetic flaws, older repairs, maybe some yellowing — but watertight and surfable. Perfect first boards, travel boards, and loaner boards. Priced to move.

Every board is leak-checked and weighed before it hits the rack, and every past repair is listed on the price tag. If we wouldn't surf it, we don't sell it.

Buying used FAQ

Good questions to ask

Are used surfboards worth buying?
Absolutely — a well-kept used board surfs exactly like it did new at 40–60% of the price. The risk with private sales is hidden waterlog and bad repairs; we eliminate that by leak-checking, weighing, and grading every board we sell.
What size board should a beginner buy in Cocoa Beach?
Cocoa Beach waves are mellow and forgiving — ideal learning surf. Most beginners do best on something 7'6"–9'0" with plenty of volume. Come in, tell us your height and weight, and we'll point you at two or three boards on the rack.
Can I trade my current board toward a used one?
Yes — bring it in for an evaluation and we'll offer trade credit on the spot. Details on our sell, trade & consign page.
How do online payments and shipping work?
Hit "Buy this board," choose pickup or shipping, then pay by card through Stripe or in crypto (BTC, ETH, USDC, and 300+ coins) through NOWPayments. Shipped boards leave the garage within 2 business days, packed with rail guards and foam, fully insured, with tracking texted to you. Local pickups get a same-day appointment window most days.
Do you hold boards?
We'll hold any board for 24 hours with a phone call. After that it goes back on the rack — inventory moves fast, especially before hurricane-season swells.
